### Game duration
- Your game should at least have 2-3+ hours of content. Anything below 2 hours is open to a no questions asked refund and possibly many negative reviews.


### Tags
- The first 5 tags are the most important. Against what is mentioned by steamworks documentation, you should NOT include every single tag your game may relate to. That will pollute non goal audience's targets, resulting in poor results and possibly reduced overall visibility.
- When first adding tags, add all tags that may be related to your game, check the review's games, check their similar games and their tags. If their similar games are better than yours, that game is better tagged and you should change yours to be more alike.
- Rearrange, add and remove checking your review for similar games and the order they were presented. Only stop when all the games in there are related and are in a proper similarity order. Then publish changes and check similar games of your game in the store (they will be different than the ones presented in review).
- AVOID:
- - "Empty callory tags": Indie; Action; Adventure; Strategy; Casual;
- - Possibly off putting tags that are not 100% relevant: Gore; Violent; Blood; Metroidvania; Specific art styles (Anime; Stylized; Cartoon)

- (July 2026) Any person, that bought your game or not, may add user tags to your game, that can eventually overwrite yours. When you publish your tags, you overwrite any user published tags, so you should retag your game every 2-3 months ideally.
- - [Blender](https://store.steampowered.com/app/365670/Blender/) has Sexual content, Hentai, Survival horror and Sandbox user defined tags.
- You can, however, 'ban' tags in your published games, by going into your game's tags > Report icon (need to confirm if it will actually ban it or just temporarily remove).

Good specific read: [How to market your game - steam 101 how to tag your game](https://howtomarketagame.com/2020/11/12/steam-101-how-to-tag-your-game/)

### Capsule art
- The most important visual aspect. If your capsule art is bad, nobody will even hover over it to see what your game is about.

Examples:
- [Bloodshed](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2747550/Bloodshed/)

### Screenshots
- The first 4 are the most important, as they appear in the carrousel when hovered over.
You should show different aspects from your game, or different art styles.
Examples: 1 for combat, 1 exploration, 1 UI (If important for your game, to show depth), upgrades/skill trees...
Things to avoid: Cutscenes, art, logo, reviews, menus,

### Short description
- Be brief, mention genre, main goal, uniqueness of your game.
- Use verbs. What will the player DO in the game? Explore, fight, craft, race,

### Trailers
First trailer:
- Should be around 1 minute, people skim through the trailer in chunks. Make it so any second of the trailer contains something that could interest the person about the genre or the game.
